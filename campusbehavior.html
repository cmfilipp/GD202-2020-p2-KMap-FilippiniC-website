CAMPUS BEHAVIOR

A major issue we face with waste management is human behavior.

The behavior of students play a major role in the ability for campuses to achieve sustainability. After interviewing students, it is clear that not only the lack of knowledge, but the convenience and overall “laziness” of students factor into their negligent behavior. In researching, I learned the importance of analyzing their behaviors and motivations for, and for not performing certain tasks and investigating ways to get them to perform additional behaviors. A recent study revealed getting students to perform sustainable behaviors require some effort to change students’ environment to help make behaviors more convenient to perform. Additonally, it reveals students are motivated by incentives, both intrinsically and extrinsically, to perform sustainably.

INTERVIEWS

Do you think you have the tools to dispense of waste properly on campus?

“I think there is definitely room for education for consumers around exactly what goes where, but I think Talley has the infrastructure there. There have been times where I have seen displays and what not. But, I think NC State is doing a good job, if they could increase awareness and education that would be good.”

- James Withrow, NC State Student

Of the waste you produce, do you dispose of it properly?

“If I am uncertain, I will put it in the trash because I am under the impression that that is better than contaminating the recycling.”

- James Withrow, NC State Student
